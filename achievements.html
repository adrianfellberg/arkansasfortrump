Income inequality

The income inequality is measured by the Gini coefficient; if it equals 0 this means there is a perfectly equal society, where everyone's income is the same. A value of 1, on the other hand, shows perfect inequality, meaning that in this case the gap between poor and rich would be immense. During Obama´s terms of office the income inequality was 0.48. After 2 years under the Trump administration income inequality has only risen by 0.02; this means that he has managed to keep the gap between poor and rich at a solid number of 0.5, which is no mean feat, considering that America had to deal with COVID-19, big forest fires and heavy flooding at the same time!

If we now take into consideration that the income inequality has not changed throughout the years of 2010-2018 but the crime rate was lowered by 9,9%, this would be a big achievement by the Trump administration since greater inequality often comes with a higher crime rate.
